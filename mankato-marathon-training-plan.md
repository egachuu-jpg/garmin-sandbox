---

## Section 1: Core Pacing Framework
Your recent 1:45 half marathon demonstrates that you possess the raw aerobic capacity to target a sub-3:40 marathon finish under normal conditions. To securely meet your sub-4 goal while protecting your right SI joint from high-impact stress, train strictly within these calculated target zones:

*   **Easy & Recovery Runs:** 10:00 to 10:45 per mile. Must feel entirely conversational to prevent structural inflammation.
*   **Marathon Goal Pace (MGP):** 9:00 to 9:09 per mile. Your target tempo and selective long-run closeout pace.
*   **Intervals / Speedwork:** 7:45 to 8:15 per mile. Used to maintain fast-twitch muscle efficiency without accumulating structural fatigue.

---

## Section 2: SI Joint Stabilization Protocol
Perform these movements on **Monday and Tuesday mornings** right before or after your easy runs. They target pelvic alignment, firing up the stabilizing muscles to keep your pelvis level under impact.

1.  **Resisted Clamshells:** 3 sets × 15 repetitions per side. Focuses on isolating the gluteus medius to stop lateral pelvic drops.
2.  **Single-Leg Glute Bridges:** 3 sets × 10 repetitions per side. Drives active hip extension through the gluteus maximus without irritating the lower lumbar vertebrae.
3.  **Bird-Dogs:** 3 sets × 12 repetitions per side. Move deliberately slowly. Emphasizes stable, cross-body core coordination.
4.  **Lateral Side Planks:** 3 sets × 30-second holds per side. Builds deep stability in the quadratus lumborum to support your pelvis on each stride.

## Section 4: Crucial Race Execution Tactics
*   **New Flatter Course Profile:** As of early 2026, Visit Mankato unveiled [faster, flatter marathon courses](https://greatermankato.com/blog/visit-mankato-unveils-faster-flatter-courses-for-2026-mankato-marathon/) for the full and half events. This removes major historic hill risks, but you must still avoid over-striding on minor downhills to prevent shear force on your right SI joint.
*   **Monday Running Rule:** Because you run back-to-back on Mondays and Tuesdays, Monday must be your slowest session of the week (10:30–11:00/mile) to preserve your joints.
*   **Bike Optimization:** Ensure your bike saddle height doesn't cause your hips to sway side-to-side. Keep cadence high and resistance low to flush out metabolic waste.
